# LabX Con 2026: Art Direction Bible, The Five Installation Universes

**Source:** LabX Con Master Deck 2026 (Refine), slides 10-21, 58-71, 96-98, 136-147
**Status:** Production reference for all LabX visual output
**Date:** 2026-07-07

LabX Con 2026 is built as five architect-designed installation islands, each with its own theme, all sharing one purpose: turning collaboration into physical form. Every island is led by one of the 5 Design Houses. The shared aesthetic instruction from the deck: "an edgy moodboard you freely to rediscover." Slightly unpolished, culturally grounded, never glossy minimalism.

---

## 1. EARTHBOUND (Sustainability)

**Positioning statement (from deck):** As sustainability becomes an increasingly important part of ESG initiatives and brand responsibility, Earthbound invites brands, collaborators, and visitors to reconnect with the earth through immersive environments inspired by nature, conscious living, and sustainable innovation. It transforms environmental awareness into an emotional, experiential, culturally relevant journey.

**Experience dimensions:** Emotional, Experiential, Cultural, Awareness. Works on the visitor both mentally and physically.

**Sensory palette:** Texture, Sound, Landscape, Aroma. This island is touched and smelled, not just seen.

**Core elements:**
1. Natural-based: dirts, water, plants, lights
2. Conscious Living: ritual and culture, material, slow lifestyle
3. Sustainable: long-term and healthy innovation
4. ESG & Brand: tanggung jawab brand/korporasi

**Physical marketing marks:** Totem, CenterSpace, Gapura, Naming.

**Design directives:** Raw natural materials over synthetic finishes. Living elements (plants, water features) integrated into structure. Lighting warm and low. Signage on natural substrate. Every material choice must survive the question "would this end up in a landfill after 3 days?"

## 2. NEXT VERSE (Technology)

**Positioning statement (from deck):** Technology is no longer just a tool. It has become part of how people communicate, shop, create, and experience the world around them. Next Verse transforms futuristic ideas into immersive playgrounds filled with interactive systems, experimental showcases, and next-generation experiences where visitors can explore technology.

**Experience dimensions:** Format, Tech, Communication, Commerce, Creation, Experience.

**Sensory palette:** Hardware, Software, Spatial, Data Viz.

**Core elements:**
1. Interactive: systems, sensor, response
2. Experimental: showcase prototype, beta version
3. Next-gen: XR, AI, Haptic experiences
4. Playground: free explore, play, discover

**Physical marketing marks:** Totem, CenterSpace, Gapura, Display.

**Design directives:** Everything responds to the visitor: sensors, triggers, real-time feedback. Prototypes and beta versions shown as-is; unfinished is a feature, not a flaw. Data visualization as spatial decoration. The island reads as a playground, not a showroom. AR overlays, VR immersion, AI personalization, and blockchain/Web3 ownership (Voyage Protocol) are the standing tech stack.

## 3. CHROMANIZE (Colorful)

**Positioning statement (from deck):** With audiences increasingly drawn to playful visuals, internet culture, collectibles, gaming, and vibrant self-expression, Chromanize transforms the island into a colorful immersive world where brands and collaborators turn imagination, creativity, and pop culture into larger-than-life experiences.

**Experience dimensions:** Memory, Sensory, Cultural, Spectacle, Nostalgia, Experience.

**Sensory palette:** Hypercolor, Sculpture, Ambience, Playful Visual.

**Core elements:**
1. Playfulness: wonder, childlike delight, interactive
2. Imagination: fantasy, creation, dreams
3. Pop Culture: nostalgia, trends, internet culture
4. Self-expression: identity, personal color style

**Physical marketing marks:** Totem, WiderSpace, Gapura, Display.

**Design directives:** Saturation turned all the way up, but within one controlled palette per zone. Oversized sculpture as landmark and photo magnet. Collectible and gaming references welcome (naming exploration in the deck flagged BRICKS/LEGO energy and "-fy"/"-nized" suffixes). This is the UGC engine of the event; every corner must be shareable.

## 4. THE ROOTS (Legacy)

**Positioning statement (from deck):** Many heritage brands face the challenge of staying relevant in a fast-moving cultural and technological landscape without losing the identity that shaped them. The Roots explores the meeting point between legacy and innovation, where craftsmanship, history, and brand values evolve into immersive experiences that grow, adapt, and inspire across generations. Alternate framing from the naming exploration: "the island where the legacy of brands meets frontier technology," blending heritage with technology to pioneer a new way of preserving brand identity in Indonesia.

**Experience dimensions:** Story, Evidence, Reverence, Continuity, Pride, Inspiration.

**Sensory palette:** Artifact, Live Craft, Material, Archive Media.

**Core elements:**
1. Legacy: history, inheritance values
2. Craftsmanship: mastery, experience, journey
3. Innovation: evolution, future-adoption
4. Brand Values: identity, core purpose

**Physical marketing marks:** Totem, WiderSpace, Gapura, Statue.

**Design directives:** Real artifacts, not reproductions. Live craftspeople working in the space; process is the exhibit. Archive photography and film as wall material. The Statue is this island's signature mark (only universe that gets one). Tone is reverent but alive, a working workshop rather than a museum vitrine.

## 5. LUCIDIA (Fantasy)

**Positioning statement (from deck):** In a market where audiences reject over-produced perfection, this installation positions the brand as a cultural leader brave enough to say that real beauty, real humanity, real connection has never been flawless. It has always been this: a little strange, a little raw, and completely, undeniably alive.

**Experience dimensions:** Vulnerability, Rawness, Belonging, Provocation, Dreams, Reality.

**Sensory palette:** Material, Absurd Visual, Voice, Imperfect Light.

**Core elements:**
1. Real Beauty: imperfect, unfiltered, anti-perfection
2. Humanity: real, flawed, vulnerable being
3. Connection: unscripted, honest
4. Bravery: culture courage, breakthrough attitude

**Physical marketing marks:** Totem, Flooring, Central Place, Display.

**Design directives:** The anti-polish island. Imperfect lighting is deliberate: exposed sources, uneven washes, shadow as material. Absurd visual compositions that provoke rather than please. Voice (spoken word, recorded confession, ambient dialogue) as a design layer. Flooring is a signature mark here; the ground plane carries the narrative. Nothing in Lucidia should look art-directed even though everything is.

Flagged in the deck's exploration slides (146-147), not yet confirmed in the main lineup:

An industrial-inspired universe where fashion, apparel, accessories, and design-driven brands explore material innovation through immersive booth experiences, experimental textures, and contemporary craftsmanship. Transforming products into bold expressions of culture and identity.

## 7. Cross-Universe Visual System

**Shared physical vocabulary.** Every island uses a Totem (vertical landmark) and a Gapura (gateway). Space type varies: CenterSpace (Earthbound, Next Verse), WiderSpace (Chromanize, The Roots), Central Place + Flooring (Lucidia).

**Reference benchmark:** Dover Street Market (named in the deck as the vibes reference). Curated retail-as-culture, each zone a distinct world under one roof.

**Layout geometry:** 5 installation islands within roughly 4,000-7,000 sqm at PIM 3 (Pondok Indah Mall 3, City Hall / Sky Level), 120-150 tenant booths from 2x2 m to 4x4 m+.

**The three brand zones (partner-facing spatial logic):**
1. **Inner Circle:** community activation hub. Get in where the real conversations happen.
2. **Culture Drop:** product showcase placed inside the experience. Don't advertise, be desired.
3. **X Lab / Lounge:** seminar, podcast, and workshop space where ideas turn live.

**Hard rules for all LabX visual output:**
- Partner brands integrate into the installation narrative; no logo-dominant placements.
- Each universe keeps its own restrained palette; the event reads cohesive, never chaotic.
- Typography carries cultural weight; no default corporate sans-serifs.
- Every space must be designed to be explored, interacted with, and shared, in that order.
- Moodboards stay edgy and rediscoverable; if it looks like a mall display, redo it.
