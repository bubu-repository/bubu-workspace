## PAGE 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Partnership Thesis
KAHF enters the Malaysian premium men's grooming market through strategic co-activation with Todak Apparel at Kuala Lumpur Fashion Week 2026. Rather than competing for standalone sponsorship, KAHF positions as Todak's lifestyle partner—facilitating model grooming, curating VIP experiences, and establishing KAHF as the grooming authority for the "cultured modern male" audience that KLFW attracts.

**Why This Works:**
- KLFW audience (47% affluent, 45% male, ages 25–44) precisely matches KAHF's core demographic
- Todak's runway runway segment (350 dedicated seats, 24 models, media coverage) provides high-visibility touchpoints
- No competitor is currently activating premium men's skincare at KLFW (first-mover advantage)
- KAHF's #1 position in Indonesia validates product credibility; KLFW is the entry point into Malaysia
- Post-event partnerships with Malaysian retailers (Watsons, Boots, Guardian) become credible after KLFW association

### Key Benefits to Each Brand

**For KAHF:**
- Market entry into Malaysia's underserved premium men's skincare category
- Positioning as "lifestyle grooming" vs. "cosmetics" (appeals to male audience)
- Reach 11,000+ affluent KLFW attendees + USD 4M earned media value
- Content/influencer gifting drives social proof in Malaysia (TikTok, Instagram)
- Retail partnership foundation for Watsons, Boots Malaysia post-KLFW

**For Todak:**
- Validates "Cultured" positioning—grooming is part of fashion lifestyle
- De-risks fashion-only perception; positions brand as broader lifestyle authority
- Provides model grooming facilitation (reduces production cost/complexity)
- Shared influencer/media gifting amplifies reach beyond fashion enthusiasts
- Strengthens narrative: "fashion + grooming = complete lifestyle"

### Success Metrics
- **Reach:** 11,000+ KLFW attendees + 12,854+ #KLFW social posts mentioning partnership
- **Engagement:** 25%+ sampling rate at activations; 500+ influencer mentions (target Malaysian beauty/lifestyle accounts)
- **Brand Lift:** Post-event awareness study (KAHF brand awareness in Malaysia; purchase intent lift)
- **Retail:** 5–10 new retailer conversations in Malaysia triggered post-KLFW (Watsons, Guardian, etc.)
- **Content:** 50+ UGC posts; 5–10 influencer pieces featuring KAHF × Todak grooming moments

- Malay majority (53%) aligns with halal-certified appeal (72% of Malaysia is Muslim)

### Indonesian Men's Skincare Market — KAHF's Momentum

**Market Position:**
- KAHF is #1 men's face care brand in Indonesia with **14.1% market share**
- Consistent double-digit growth; outpacing women's skincare category
- Awards: #1 at Shopee, Tokopedia, Watsons, Guardian, across multiple retailers

**Product Strength:**
- Halal-certified, natural formulations (resonates with Muslim consumer base in Malaysia)
- Comprehensive grooming ecosystem: face care, body care, fragrance, hairstyle products
- Pricing: Premium positioning (15–25% below Kiehl's/Aesop, perceived as better value)

### Malaysian Men's Skincare Market — The Opportunity

**Market Dynamics:**
- Men's grooming in Malaysia: **8–12% CAGR (2019–2023); projected 10–15% (2024–2027)**
- Online channels dominate: **60%+ of grooming sales on Lazada, Shopee, TikTok Shop**
- Primary buyer age: **25–45** (matches KLFW demographic exactly)

**Competitive Landscape:**
- **Mass market:** Men's Biore (dominant), Nivea Men, Ponds Men
- **Premium international:** Kiehl's, Aesop, L'Oréal Men Expert
- **K-beauty:** Laneige Men (growing), Some By Mi
- **Indonesian competitors in Malaysia:** Wardah Men (underperforming), Emina (women-focused)

**Critical Insight:** There is **no strong competitor for "premium Indonesian men's skincare"** in Malaysia. KAHF enters an underserved niche with first-mover advantage.

1. **Audience concentration:** 11,000 affluent, educated, style-conscious attendees in one venue
2. **Media amplification:** USD 4.06M earned media value; Says.com (5M visitors), Hype.My (1.7M), Tatlerasia, Augustman reaching lifestyle-conscious Malaysians
3. **Credibility via association:** Todak partnership (fashion designer credibility) elevates KAHF from "e-commerce brand" to "lifestyle partner"
4. **Halal messaging authority:** Fashion week audience understands premium positioning + halal-certified appeal
5. **Post-event retail momentum:** KLFW visibility enables conversations with Watsons, Boots Malaysia, Guardian for broader distribution

## PAGE 4: STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT & ACTIVATION PLAN

#### CONCEPT 1: KAHF Backstage Grooming Bar (Model Prep Facilitation)
**Positioning:** "Professional grooming for the runway"

**Execution:**
- KAHF sponsors dedicated grooming station backstage during Todak segment
- KAHF grooming specialist oversees model prep (skin touch-ups, beard grooming, scalp treatment)
- All 24 Todak models use KAHF products pre-runway
- Visible signage: "Models groomed with KAHF" (understated, professional)

**Content Moments:**
- Behind-the-scenes photos: models being groomed → styled → runway walk
- Influencer access: beauticians, fashion journalists, model companions see KAHF in action
- TikTok/Reels: "The grooming behind the runway" 15–30 sec clips

**Budget Implication:** Low-to-medium effort (20–40M IDR)
- 1 KAHF grooming specialist (fee + airfare + accommodation)

- Signage and photography

**ROI:** High visibility, authentic integration, drives credibility narrative

### Critical Unknowns to Verify (Honest Assessment)

Before finalizing, we recommend confirming with KLFW organizing team:

1. **Sponsorship approval:** Does KAHF need formal "category sponsor" status, or can it partner designer-specific (via Todak)?
2. **Backstage access:** Is backstage grooming facilitation standard for sponsors, or requires special negotiation?
3. **Branding restrictions:** Specific rules for logo placement, signage sizes, proximity to runway?
4. **Makeup/grooming conflicts:** Is there an existing hair/makeup partner? Any exclusivity restrictions?
5. **Designer autonomy:** How much control does Todak have over backstage activities vs. KLFW directive?
6. **Timeline:** What's the latest submission date for sponsorship proposals? (Likely June–July 2026)

**Why this matters:** The difference between casual partnership and formal sponsorship affects budget, logistics, and activation permissions. These constraints are normal at fashion events; we're flagging them to show we understand the "unwritten rules."
