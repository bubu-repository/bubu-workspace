# Korean Beauty (K-Beauty) for Men in Malaysia
## Market Research Report
**Date:** June 29, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The Malaysian men's skincare market is experiencing significant growth, with Korean beauty brands establishing strong market penetration. Laneige Men and Cosrx represent two distinct positioning strategies within this segment—premium hydration-focused (Laneige) and affordable, ingredient-driven (Cosrx). The K-beauty sector for men in Malaysia is driven by Gen Z consumers, growing image-consciousness among urban males, and the cultural influence of K-pop and K-dramas. Market size in Malaysia's broader skincare category reached USD 804.85 million in 2025, with men's grooming expected to grow at 9.2% CAGR during the forecast period.

## 5. Target Consumer Segments in Malaysia

### Primary Segments for K-Beauty Men's Products

#### 1. **Urban Young Professionals (Millennials & Gen Z)**
- **Age:** 20-35 years old
- **Location:** Kuala Lumpur, Penang, urban centers
- **Characteristics:**
  - Higher disposable income
  - Image-conscious, influenced by K-pop and K-drama
  - Active on social media
  - Seek scientific efficacy with visible results
  - Value natural/clean formulations
  - Price-conscious but willing to pay for proven efficacy
- **Preferred brands:** Laneige (premium), Cosrx (value), Innisfree (mid-range)

#### 2. **K-Pop/K-Drama Enthusiasts**
- **Age:** 15-30 years old
- **Characteristics:**
  - Cultural influence from Korean entertainment
  - Aspire to Korean beauty standards
  - Early adopters of K-beauty trends
  - Social media-driven purchasing
  - Influenced by celebrity endorsements
  - Growing male segment
- **Market Impact:** K-pop and K-drama cited as key drivers of K-beauty popularity in Malaysia

#### 3. **Health-Conscious, Ingredient-Focused Consumers**
- **Age:** 25-40 years old
- **Characteristics:**
  - Research-driven purchasing decisions
  - Compare clinical claims and ingredient lists
  - View skincare as self-investment
  - Prefer transparent brands
  - Seek natural/organic options
  - Appreciate scientific backing
- **Preferred segment:** Cosrx (ingredient transparency), Laneige (clinical claims)
- **Trend:** 54% of Gen Z seek natural, clean formulations for tropical climate skin concerns

#### 4. **Value-for-Money Seekers**
- **Age:** 18-35 years old
- **Characteristics:**
  - Budget-conscious
  - Want premium-quality at affordable prices
  - Appreciate compact, efficient packaging
  - 96% Gen Z have purchased skincare in last 3-6 months
  - 54% keen on trying new, unique products
- **Preferred brands:** Cosrx, Innisfree, Some by Mi
- **Insight:** K-beauty appeals to Southeast Asian consumers' price sensitivity while maintaining efficacy perception

#### 5. **Premium Segment (Affluent Males)**
- **Age:** 30-50 years old
- **Characteristics:**
  - High disposable income
  - Seek premium, luxury positioning
  - Value brand heritage and scientific research
  - Less price-sensitive
  - Appreciate minimalist, sophisticated packaging
- **Preferred brands:** Aesop, IOPE Men, Premium Laneige sets
- **Note:** Smaller segment, but high lifetime value

### Geographic Focus
- **High-penetration areas:** Kuala Lumpur, Penang, Johor Bahru

## 7. Consumer Behavior & Brand Preferences

### Key Behavioral Insights

#### Purchase Decision Drivers
1. **Ingredient Efficacy** (Scientific approach)
   - Korean brands highlight clinical research
   - 25% of Malaysian men already using Korean products
   - Appeal to consumers who compare claims and evaluate ingredients

2. **Celebrity & Influencer Influence**
   - K-pop celebrities shape brand perception
   - Social media driving purchasing decisions among Gen Z
   - YouTube reviews and beauty influencers key touchpoints

3. **Price-to-Efficacy Ratio**
   - Southeast Asian consumers price-conscious but willing to invest in proven efficacy
   - K-beauty appeals through premium quality at accessible prices
   - Discount/promotional sensitivity high (Shopee/Lazada campaigns)

4. **Natural/Clean Ingredients**
   - 54% of Gen Z seeking natural, clean formulations
   - Tropical climate drives interest in hydrating, protective ingredients
   - Asian beauty concern with skin brightening + protection

5. **Brand Personality & Loyalty**
   - Brand personality strongly influences brand preference and loyalty
   - Intangible attributes (heritage, values, aesthetic) drive loyalty
   - Once consumers adopt a K-beauty brand, repeat purchase likelihood is high

#### Market Risk Factors (Malaysia-Specific)
- Religious/cultural considerations: Some conflict perception between Korean culture and Islamic values (noted among Malaysian consumers)
- Market differences vs. Indonesia: Malaysian consumers more receptive to K-beauty, fewer cultural barriers

#### Trend: Premium at Affordable Price
- K-beauty brands winning market share by offering "premium-quality skincare at affordable prices"
- Malaysian consumers particularly value this value proposition
- E-commerce enabling this through reducing distribution markups

## 9. Pricing Strategy Analysis

### Laneige Men Pricing Strategy
- **Premium positioning:** RM 140-180 for core moisturizers
- **Value-added bundles:** RM 230+ for sets (increases perceived value)
- **Entry-point products:** RM 70-80 toners/cleansers (reduce barrier to trial)
- **Strategy:** Tiered pricing to capture different consumer segments while maintaining premium image
- **Psychological pricing:** Set price points (RM 71.90, RM 79.90, RM 140.90) suggest value without "cheap" perception

### Cosrx Pricing Strategy
- **Ultra-competitive:** RM 21-80 range
- **Flagship product pricing:** Advanced Snail 96 at RM 54-61 (affordable efficacy leader)
- **Promotional aggressiveness:** Observed discounts (RM 69.90→RM 57.90) indicate volume-driven strategy
- **Strategy:** Low-price, high-volume to build market share and brand loyalty
- **Value perception:** Affordable entry point invites trial; efficacy drives repeat purchase

### Strategic Insight
- **Laneige:** Premium brand justified by proprietary technology, heritage, and Sephora positioning
- **Cosrx:** Volume-driven, accessibility-focused, rapid market penetration strategy
- **Market gap:** Clear differentiation allows both brands to thrive without direct price competition
