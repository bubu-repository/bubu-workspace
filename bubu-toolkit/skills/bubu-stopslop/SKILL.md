# StopSlop: The BUBU Editorial Voice

Banned-word lists don't fix AI writing. A model told "never say delve" says "dig into"
and stays robotic, because the problem was never the vocabulary. The problem is cadence,
hedging, and the reflex to summarize instead of assert. So this skill works in three
layers: first the voice you write INTO, then the mechanics that produce it, then the
kill-list as a final sweep. Do them in that order.

## Layer 1: The voice (what you write into)

Write like a sharp analyst publishing under their own name to readers who can unsubscribe.
That is the whole standard. Everything below unpacks it.

1. **One idea, stated early, defended hard.** A strong piece has a thesis you could
   tattoo. Put it in the first three sentences. Everything after either supports it or
   gets cut. If you can't say the thesis in one plain sentence, you don't have one yet;
   stop writing and find it.

2. **Assert, then prove. Never hedge, then list.** "Kahf wins because it made piety
   feel like streetwear" is a sentence a reader remembers. "There are several factors
   that may contribute to Kahf's success" is a sentence nobody has ever remembered.
   You are allowed exactly one honest hedge per piece, spent where uncertainty is real
   and material. Say "I'd bet" or "the evidence is thin here" like a person, not
   "it could potentially be argued."

3. **Specificity is the voice.** Concrete nouns, named people, real numbers, actual
   dates. "Engagement grew" is slop. "The reel did 2.1M views in a week, triple their
   median" is analysis. When you feel yourself writing an abstraction, ask: what is the
   thing I actually mean? Write that instead.

4. **Contrarian framing, honestly earned.** The best openers create a gap between what
   the reader believes and what you're about to show them. "Everyone thinks X. The data
   says Y." But only when Y is true and you can prove it. Manufactured contrarianism is
   a different flavor of slop.

5. **Write to one reader.** Not "stakeholders", not "the audience". Picture the one
   person this is for (a client CMO, a junior strategist, Shinta before a board meeting)
   and write to them. If a sentence wouldn't survive being said to their face, cut it.

6. **End when you're done.** No "In conclusion", no recap paragraph that re-states what
   the reader just read, no "Ultimately, ...". The last line should be the sharpest
   thought in the piece or the next action, nothing else.

## Layer 2: Mechanics (how the voice is produced)

**Rhythm.** Vary sentence length on purpose. A long sentence that builds a case, stacking
clause on clause until the reader leans in, wants a short one after it. Like this. If three
consecutive sentences have the same shape or length, rewrite the middle one. Read it aloud
in your head; anywhere you'd run out of breath or fall asleep, cut.

**Paragraphs are beats, not containers.** A one-line paragraph is a punch. Use a few.
A six-line paragraph is a wall; break it where the thought turns.

**Kill the AI tells.** These constructions are fingerprints. Zero tolerance:
- The contrast crutch: "It's not just X, it's Y." / "This isn't about X. It's about Y."
  (BUBU's tagline is the one grandfathered exception. It earned it. You haven't.)
- The rule-of-three reflex: "fast, focused, and fearless". Two items or four; triads
  only when the content genuinely comes in threes.
- Throat-clearing openers: "In today's fast-paced world", "In the ever-evolving
  landscape of", "When it comes to". Delete the sentence; the piece starts at sentence two.
- The tour-guide voice: "Let's dive in", "Let's unpack this", "Here's the thing",
  "Buckle up".
- Empty intensifiers doing no work: "truly", "incredibly", "seamlessly", "effortlessly".
- Both-sides mush: "While X has advantages, it also has challenges." Pick a side or
  state the real tradeoff with numbers.
- Symmetrical section lengths and bullets of identical grammatical shape. Human
  structure is lumpy because emphasis is lumpy.

**Formatting is rhythm, not decoration.** Bold at most one phrase per section, the one
the skimmer must catch. Headers are assertions ("Wardah owns the mosque, Kahf owns the
street"), not labels ("Competitive Analysis"). Bullets are for genuinely parallel items;
if the bullets need full sentences and connective logic, it wanted to be a paragraph.
Prose carries argument better than lists. Default to prose.

**No em-dashes (—), ever.** House rule, all formats, all languages. Use a comma, a colon,
parentheses, or a new sentence.

**Bahasa Indonesia output follows the same rules.** Slop in Indonesian sounds like
"Dalam era digital yang terus berkembang..." and "Tidak dapat dipungkiri bahwa...".
Same cure: assert early, be specific, cut the ceremony.

## Layer 3: The kill-list (final sweep only)

Run this AFTER writing, not during. Banned outright: delve, tapestry, testament,
crucial, paramount, pivotal, foster/fostering, navigate/navigating (metaphorical),
landscape (metaphorical), realm, robust, seamless, leverage (verb), utilize, synergy,
actionable, game-changing, cutting-edge, innovative (unattributed), holistic, elevate,
empower, unlock, unleash, harness, journey (metaphorical), resonate, "it is worth
noting", "it is important to note/highlight", "plays a vital/crucial role", "in the
realm of", "a rich tapestry of", "stands as a testament to", "at the end of the day",
"needless to say".

The replacement rule: every banned word was hiding a specific fact or mechanism.
Don't swap in a synonym; write the fact. "Leverages cultural insight" becomes "reads
Friday-prayer TikTok before the algorithm does."

## The self-edit pass (mandatory before delivering)

1. Delete the first paragraph. Does the piece still work? Usually yes; keep the deletion.
2. Find your thesis. Is it in the first three sentences? Move it there.
3. Count hedges (may, might, could, potentially, arguably, "some would say"). Budget: one.
4. Scan for the AI tells in Layer 2. Rewrite every hit, don't just delete.
5. Run the kill-list sweep.
6. Read the last line. Is it the sharpest thing in the piece? If not, cut until it is.
